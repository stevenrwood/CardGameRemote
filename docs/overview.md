# Card Game Remote

## What Is It?

Card Game Remote lets someone play poker at your table from hundreds of
miles away. They see their cards on their computer, hear everything through
a video call, and play just like they were sitting right there.

## Why?

Our poker group has been meeting monthly for 35 years. When one of our
players moved to Texas, we didn't want to lose him from the game. This
system lets him join every game night as if he never left.

## How Does It Work?

A small box sits on the poker table. When the dealer gives the remote
player a face-down card, they slide it into one of seven slots on top of
the box. A camera inside the box reads the card and instantly sends it to
the remote player's screen. Nobody at the table can see what his cards are.

For face-up cards, the dealer just deals them normally and calls them out
loud as always. The software listens and picks up what was said.

When it's time to trade in cards (like in a draw game), the remote player
clicks the cards he wants to get rid of. Small green lights on the box
show the dealer which cards to pull out and replace.

The remote player watches the table through a video call on Teams. He can
see the other players, hear the conversation, and announce his bets and
moves just by talking. His face shows up on a screen at the table so
everyone can see him too.

A second camera above the table can read the other players' face-up cards
and show them on the remote player's screen, so he never has to ask
"what's showing?"

## What Games Can It Handle?

All of our regular games: 5 Card Draw, 7 Card Stud, Follow the Queen,
High Chicago, 3 Toed Pete, High/Low/High Challenge, 7/27, Deuces Wild,
and Texas Hold'em. The software knows the rules for each game and
automatically handles whether a card is face-up or face-down.

## What Do You Need?

- A laptop at the table (runs the software and the video call)
- A small card-reading box (about the size of a shoebox lid, 2 inches tall)
- A screen for the remote player's face (so the table can see him)
- A phone for the dealer (to control which game is being played)
- A camera above the table (reads what cards are showing)
- The remote player just needs a computer with Teams
